Cooking Mindset

Description

This first recipe isnt one. It is a philosophy, or a mindset of how I approach cooking. I learned this over the years of cooking for myself and others.

If you look at cooking every meal as a set of instructions to keep catalogued in your head, you can quickly get bored uninspired or discouraged. The goal of cooking is to make good food that you enjoy everyday. It is not to be the best at following a set of instructions. Do not be afraid to be creative or change some ingredients when and if you cannot find them.

In light of that, my advice is to learn how to cook the staples really well, then you learn how to mix the universal flavors. That is all that cooking is. Once you know this, you can mix and match anything you want without having to revert to google or youtube everytime you get hungry

Strong Staples To Learn

  • How to cook chicken
  • How to cook red meat
  • How to roast Vegetables
  • learn one or two desserts
  • Rice
  • Potatoes
  • Eggs

Some Important Concepts

  • Learn about flavors: salt, sweet, fat, spice, acid, umami, etc...
  • Basic salad dressings
  • Mother sauces
  • Marinades
  • Proper cutting technique
  • Basic baking: crepes, pancakes, cookies

Conclusion

Once you know all this, no dish will be out of your reach. More importantly, you will not need to find a new recipe every time that you are cooking food. You will be able to open any fridge or pantry and quickly come up with ideas on what to make for dinner!
